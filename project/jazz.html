Jazz Legends

Explore the lives and music of jazz's greatest legends

Louis Armstrong

Louis Armstrong, also known as "Satchmo," was an American trumpeter, composer, vocalist, and actor who was one of the most influential figures in jazz. Armstrong's career spanned five decades, and he is known for his distinctively gravelly voice and improvisational skills.

Best Known For: "What a Wonderful World", "Hello, Dolly!", "La Vie En Rose"

John Coltrane

John Coltrane was an American jazz saxophonist and composer who became one of the most significant figures in jazz history. Coltrane's innovative approach to improvisation and his explorations of modal jazz and avant-garde techniques have left a lasting impact on the genre.

Best Known For: "My Favorite Things", "Giant Steps", "A Love Supreme"

Frank Sinatra

Frank Sinatra, also known as "Ol' Blue Eyes," was an American singer, actor, and producer who was one of the most popular and influential musical artists of the 20th century. Sinatra's smooth, baritone voice and impeccable phrasing made him a beloved icon of jazz and popular music.

Best Known For: "My Way", "Fly Me to the Moon", "New York, New York"
